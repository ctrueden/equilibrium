Back at [Grohnea](../dossiers/grohnea)'s cottage, in the aftermath of [Sissae](../dossiers/sissae-moondrop)'s death and partial(?) restoration, the agents were finally relieved of the [Fluxtaint](../locales/flux) warping their souls:

- [Oz](../dossiers/oz)'s idealized self image: *Dope ass swashbuckling halfling with no horns or sparky feet who moves at the speed of hobbit, Dean of Stabington Universities school of Stabbings, rocking out with ye olde booze while doing backflips on a pirate ship.*
- [Freki](../dossiers/freki)'s idealized self image: *Free of the killing curses, at peace with not only [Nature](../dossiers/gaia), but with **my** nature. At peace with [Hugin](../dossiers/hugin) and [Munin](../dossiers/munin), understanding the meaning of their words, rather than just **hoping** I interpreted them correctly.*
- Freki's internalized fear: *I am moving in the wrong world, for the right world, but accepted by neither. Woodfolk don't like me because I spend too much time in the city and dealing with things that are not Natural Law. "Civilization" doesn't like me because I'm different. Somehow, in a world of monsters, I am the monster.* Freki sees Cursed Freki almost as a separate entity. His actions feel a bit like watching a movie from the inside. He's afraid that he doesn't like himself, but he accepts that if he has to live with Evil Freki to keep his woods safe, that's a deal with the devil he'll take.

Michael rolls a nat 20 on a Wisdom save to focus on the ideal version of himself rather than his fears and internalized negativity. He emerges from the curse extractio feeling a deep sense of well-being and oneness he has not approached since he meditated with his father decades ago to commune with the beast within. He knows in his bones that&mdash;far from being pawns of the [Raven Queen](../dossiers/nerull)&mdash;Hugin and Munin are ancient allies steering the course of history since the world was young: instruments of [Gaia](../dossiers/gaia)'s primal authority and universal oneness. They are important and their connection to Freki is fated.

The agents then spent the afternoon and evening recuperating at Grohnea's cottage from their intense day of giant ass-kickery and curse-extraction exertions.

Freki collaborated with Grohnea to design new kinds of arrows:

* 3x - LV 13 ARROW - Treant Sap Arrow — Tipped with hardened ancient treant sap. On impact, it releases a sticky resin that coats the target's feet, reducing their movement speed by half and leaving a trail a skilled tracker can follow for hours.
* 2x - Razorvine Arrow — Fletched with actual razorvine leaves, preserved stiff. The arrow leaves a trail of razor-edged cuttings in flight; any creature passing through its path takes slashing damage from the debris cloud (hangs in air for 1d4 rounds).
* 3x - Black Lotus Head — A rare and dangerous arrow tipped with crystallized black lotus extract. The target must succeed on a Constitution save or fall Unconscious for 1 minute. Valuable, hard to make, and morally complicated — definitely the kind of thing the SPI would regulate heavily.
* 2x - Verdant Bloom Arrow — From Sissae's bloom curse. On impact, causes rapid plant growth from the wound site — flowers and vines bloom across the target's body. Mechanically what happens is unknown...

Oz listened surreptitiously, then secured vials of Black Lotus and Belladonna from Grohnea, as well as a simpler poisoner's kit,

### Plants/arrows Freki did not pursue

* Assassin Vine Arrow — The arrowhead is carved from a dried vine cutting. On hit, it erupts into fast-growing tendrils that attempt to grapple and restrain the target (replicates the Ensnaring Strike spell effect, no concentration needed).
* Belladonna Head — The arrowhead is hollow, filled with powdered nightshade. On impact it releases a cloud of toxic dust causing the Poisoned condition and imposing disadvantage on Wisdom saves (fitting against lycanthropes, who are weakened by belladonna).
* Ironwood Spike — Dense ironwood with no tip; it's blunt and magically hardened. Deals bludgeoning damage and hits as hard as a warhammer — useful against skeletons and constructs where piercing damage is less effective.
* Mistletoe Arrow — Fashioned by druids at solstice, the shaft is wrapped in dried mistletoe sacred to nature gods. It bypasses magical protections that are plant or nature-derived (such as barkskin or natural armor of plant creatures), treating them as unarmored.
* Thornwood Bolt — Made from the thorned heartwood of a blackthorn tree sacred to the Feywild. The arrow splinters into dozens of bone-like thorns inside the wound, making it painful to remove — the target takes damage on any strenuous movement until healed.
* Witch Hazel Whistler — Carved from witch hazel, which is traditionally associated with dowsing and revelation. When fired, it emits a harmonic whistle that reveals the location of invisible creatures in a 30-foot radius as they react to the sound. No damage, pure utility.
* Yggdrasil Splinter Arrow — Carved from a splinter of a World Tree branch. On a hit, the target is briefly connected to the web of life — the ranger becomes aware of the target's current HP, condition, and general emotional state until the start of their next turn. Pure information.
